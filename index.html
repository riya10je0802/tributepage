Kalpana Chawla was born on 17 March 1962 in karnal, East Punjab state ,India and she was brought up in United States. She was an American astronaut and mechanical engineer. Her first mission was Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997 as a specialist and primary robotic arm operator and thus she became first indian born woman to go to space.

  • In 1988,Chawla began working at NASA Ames Research Centre,where she did computational fluid dynamics research on vertical and/or short take off and landing concepts.

  • After becoming a neutralized U.S. citizen in April 1991, Chawla applied for the NASA Astronaut Corps. She joined the corps in March 1995 and was selected for her first flight in 1997.

  • Kalpana's first space mission began on 19th November 1997 as part of the six-astronaut crew that flew the Space Shuttle Columbia flight STS-87.

  • She spoke the following words while travelling in the wieghtlessness of space,"You are just your intelligence".

  • After the completion of STS-87 post -flight activities,Chawla was assigned to technical positions in the astronaut office to work on the space station.

  • Chawla died on 1 February 2003,in the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster,along with the other six crew members,when Columbia disintergrated over Texas during re-entry into the Earth's atmosphere,shortly before it was scheduled to conclude its 28th mission,STS-107.
